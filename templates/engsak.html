    Űlemiste city's history

    In 1899, one of the largest railway plants in Estonia started operations. In 1931, the company went bankrupt and created an airport instead of the country. after the war, the rebuilding of the factory begins, various people from the Soviet Union are being brought in. During the Cold War, the Factory produced a variety of experimental weapons and techniques for the space program. After the collapse of the Soviet Union, the company went bankrupt, a couple of years later the terotorium was privatised by Mainor AS. In 2005, the idea of creating a smart town emerged.

    Facts about Estonia

    Estonia has the fourth smallest population in the European Union, and the eighth smallest land area.
    The country is flat as a pancake - its highest peak is 318 metres above sea level - and it is also the highest in the Baltic states
